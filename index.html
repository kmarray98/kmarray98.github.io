Kieran Marray

I am a Phd candidate at Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam, working on econommics and econometrics of networks. My supervisors are Dr Michael Konig at Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam, and Prof Ozan Candogan at the University of Chicago.

Selected research
Estimating optimal disease mitigation with endogenous network response

We introduce a SIR-type model of the spread of disease on a social network where susceptibles rewire links away from the infected. With rewiring, optimal lockdowns are shorter, more targeted and more efficient. We introduce a simulated moments estimator to estimate parameters when rewiring is unobserved. As an illustration, we estimate counterfactual optimal policy for the Netherlands during the second wave of COVID-19.
